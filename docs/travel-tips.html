Travel Tips

Hard-won, practical advice to help you have a smooth, good time on the road.

Destinations Solo Travel Travel Tips Activities to try

Here are some general recommendations to help you have a good time.

1

Arrange and book ahead

Booking early gets you better deals. Before you travel, learn about the culture, transportation, and sights of your destination.

2

Pack smartly and light

Take only what you need and outfits that work multiple ways — you'll save time and energy. Dress in layers for all conditions.

  • Pack power banks and chargers; bring converters or adapters for other countries.
3

Keep vital papers safe

Copy your passport, ID, and trip insurance, and keep the copies separate from the originals.

  • For valuables, use a money belt or concealed pouch.
4

Money matters

  • Tell your bank about your trip so your cards aren't suspended.
  • Carry both cash and cards — not everywhere takes cards, and small cash sums are sometimes preferred.
5

Take care of your health

Drink plenty of water, especially when flying or somewhere hot. If tap water isn't safe, stick to bottled.

  • Bring any medications you need and know what care is available locally.
  • Consider travel insurance in case you fall ill.
6

Use technology

  • Use offline maps (Google Maps offline or Maps.me) when data is limited.
  • Use Google Translate and TripIt for foreign languages and itineraries.
  • Avoid roaming fees with a travel plan or local SIM.
7

Respect different cultures

Learn a few basic words — a simple "hello" or "thank you" goes a long way. Check the local tipping customs.

8

Eat locally

Take the chance to sample new foods, but avoid street food and unprotected water to prevent stomach trouble.

9

Adjust to the time zone

Shift your bedtime a few days before you leave to ease jet lag. Stay active during the day and get sunlight to help your body adjust.
